WORLD OF WARCRAFT: MIDNIGHT
COOKING LEVELING GUIDE (1–100) & BEST FOOD BUFFS
========================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This guide covers how to level Cooking from 1 to 100 in Midnight using the fastest and
cheapest path available, along with the best food buffs for feasts, master recipes, and
budget options.

The majority of the leveling path uses vendor-purchased materials available near the Cooking
trainer in Silvermoon City, so you do not need to visit the Auction House to level.


COOKING TRAINER
---------------
The Midnight Cooking trainer is Sylann, located inside the inn in Silvermoon City. Talk to
her to learn Cooking and to pick up new recipes as your skill increases.


LEVELING PATH: SKILL 1 TO 35
------------------------------
Craft Spiced Biscuits until the recipe turns grey (around skill 35).

Materials required:
- A Big Ol' Stick of Butter: 200
- Pouch of Spices: 600

Both materials are sold by the Cooking supply vendor near your trainer in Silvermoon City.
Do not buy these from the Auction House as the vendor price will be cheaper.

Note: If you are only leveling Cooking to unlock the Deftness, Finesse, or Perception food
buff recipes for gathering professions, you can stop at skill 25.


LEVELING PATH: SKILL 35 TO 100
--------------------------------
At skill 35 you unlock the Hearty Food recipe. This recipe uses crafted food as its material
input, meaning you can use the Spiced Biscuits you have already been making. This allows you
to level all the way to 100 using only vendor-purchased materials.

The recipe will be green (reduced skill gain) for approximately the last 25 skill points, so
expect to craft more than usual during that stretch. It remains cheap and fast regardless.

Materials required for this phase:
- A Big Ol' Stick of Butter: 800
- Pouch of Spices: 2400
These produce 200 Spiced Biscuits, which are then used as the input for Hearty Food.

If you have not reached skill 100 after using all your Spiced Biscuits, craft more Spiced
Biscuits and continue applying Hearty Food until you hit 100.


RECIPE SOURCES
---------------
Flora Frenzy is a reward from the Gomphusta quest in Harandar.
All other recipes listed in this guide are learned from Sylann, the Cooking trainer in
Silvermoon City.


BEST FOOD BUFFS: FEASTS
------------------------
Feasts buff every member of your group simultaneously. All feasts in Midnight grant +98
Stamina in addition to their primary stat bonus.

Blooming Feast — Grants +65 Highest Secondary Stat and +98 Stamina.
Materials: 5x Mana Lily, 5x Azeroot, 5x Argentleaf, 40x Plant Protein, 2x Petrified Root.

Harandar Celebration — Grants +50 Primary Stat and +98 Stamina.
Materials: 3x Azeroot, 40x Plant Protein, 12x Mote of Wild Magic, 2x Petrified Root.

Quel'dorei Medley — Grants +65 Highest Secondary Stat and +98 Stamina.
Materials: 24x Eversong Trout, 18x Restored Songfish, 40x Thalassian Filet, 2x Petrified Root.

Silvermoon Parade — Grants +50 Primary Stat and +98 Stamina.
Materials: 18x Lynxfish, 18x Arcane Wyrmfish, 40x Practically Pork, 2x Petrified Root.


BEST FOOD BUFFS: MASTER RECIPES
---------------------------------
These individual food buffs use master recipes and provide higher stat values than the cheaper
alternatives below.

Champion's Bento — +65 Highest Secondary Stat.
Materials: 20x Practically Pork, 20x Plant Protein, 20x Thalassian Filet.

Flora Frenzy — +65 Highest Secondary Stat.
Materials: 50x Plant Protein. (Recipe from Gomphusta quest in Harandar.)

Impossibly Royal Roast — +65 Primary Stat.
Materials: 60x Plant Protein, 16x Ripened Vegetable Assortment.

Royal Roast — +65 Primary Stat.
Materials: 60x Practically Pork, 16x Ripened Vegetable Assortment.


BEST FOOD BUFFS: CHEAPER ALTERNATIVES
---------------------------------------
These advanced recipes provide slightly lower stats (+59 instead of +65) but are intended to
be cheaper to craft. Note that some fish-heavy recipes may end up costing as much as or more
than the master recipes depending on fish prices on your realm, so compare costs before choosing.

Critical Strike Food:
- Arcano Cutlets (+59 Critical Strike) — 20x Practically Pork.
- Sun-Seared Lumifin (+59 Critical Strike) — 8x Tender Lumifin.
- Tasty Smoked Tetra (+59 Critical Strike) — 24x Twisted Tetra, 8x Mote of Primal Energy.

Haste Food:
- Crimson Calamari (+59 Haste) — 6x Sanguithorn, 24x Ominous Octopus.
- Fel-Kissed Filet (+59 Haste) — 20x Thalassian Filet.
- Null and Void Plate (+59 Haste) — 8x Warping Wise, 4x Ominous Octopus.

Mastery Food:
- Glitter Skewers (+59 Mastery) — 8x Shimmersiren, 20x Thalassian Filet.
- Warped Wise Wings (+59 Mastery) — 12x Warping Wise.

Versatility Food:
- Braised Blood Hunter (+59 Versatility) — 24x Blood Hunter, 8x Mote of Primal Energy.
- Buttered Root Crab (+59 Versatility) — 24x Root Crab, 12x Plant Protein.
- Void-Kissed Fish Rolls (+59 Versatility) — 16x Thalassian Filet, 8x Ripened Vegetable Assortment.


PROFESSION EQUIPMENT
---------------------
Cooking uses two profession equipment slots. Neither is crafted by Cooking itself — the tool
comes from Inscription and the accessory comes from Tailoring. Green quality items can be
purchased from the Auction House. Blue and Epic quality items are Bind on Pickup and must be
obtained through Crafting Orders.

Tool (Inscription):
- Green: Hobbyist Rolling Pin
- Blue (Rare): Sin'dorei Rolling Pin
- Epic: Gilded Sin'dorei Rolling Pin

Accessory — Hat (Tailoring):
- Green: Chef's Bright Linen Cooking Chapeau
- Blue: Elegant Artisan's Cooking Hat
- Epic: Thalassian Chef's Chapeau
