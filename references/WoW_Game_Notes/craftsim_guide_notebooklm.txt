WORLD OF WARCRAFT
CRAFTSIM ADDON GUIDE
========================================
Last Updated: January 14, 2026

OVERVIEW
--------
CraftSim is the only addon that incorporates your actual profession stats when calculating
crafting profits. This makes it an essential tool for any serious crafter, unless you manage
all your crafting decisions through a personal spreadsheet. Combined with TradeSkillMaster,
CraftSim is one of the most powerful addon setups available for gold-making through crafting.

This guide covers initial setup and all the major features: Average Profit, Simulation Mode,
Specialization Info, Price Overrides, and Recipe Scan.


INITIAL SETUP
--------------
After downloading and installing CraftSim, type /craftsim in the chat window to open the
settings panel.

Price Source: Set your price source to TradeSkillMaster for the most reliable pricing data.
Auctionator is also a valid option, but it is more susceptible to artificially low bait-priced
listings on the Auction House, which can result in falsely optimistic profit estimates. If you
use Auctionator, make sure you scan the Auction House before every crafting session.

All other settings can be left at their defaults.


FEATURE: AVERAGE PROFIT
------------------------
When you open your Profession window, a CraftSim button appears in the top left. The only
option you need to enable here is Average Profit. This calculates your expected profit per
craft using your actual profession stats, which is what makes CraftSim more accurate than
generic profit calculators.

Important limitations to be aware of:
- Average Profit is not accurate for all craft types. Milling and unravelling will never show
  a profit in CraftSim even when they are genuinely profitable. However, if a craft does show
  profit, that number is reliable as long as Auction House prices have not changed since your
  last scan.

How material rank affects the calculation: CraftSim looks at the materials you currently have
in your bags and uses those ranks in its calculation. If you have Quality 2 materials, it uses
Q2. If you have Quality 3, it uses Q3. If you have a mix, it defaults to the lowest rank
present, unless you check the "Use Max Rank Materials" checkbox in the CraftSim window.


FEATURE: SIMULATION MODE
--------------------------
If you do not have the required materials in your bags, or if you want to compare profitability
across different material ranks without actually owning them, use Simulation Mode.

In Simulation Mode you can manually adjust the rank of each material input and the Average
Profit window will update in real time to reflect those changes. This is useful for planning
purchases or evaluating whether upgrading to higher quality materials is worth the cost.


FEATURE: SPECIALIZATION INFO
------------------------------
Tick the "Specialization Info" option in CraftSim and a new popup window will appear. This
window shows you exactly which profession knowledge points and specialization nodes affect the
currently selected craft, what your current stat values are for that craft, and what the
maximum possible stats are. This lets you confirm whether you are fully optimized for a
particular craft before committing materials.


FEATURE: PRICE OVERRIDES
--------------------------
Price Overrides allow you to substitute a custom material cost in place of the Auction House
price for any reagent. This is one of the most powerful features in CraftSim for serious crafters.

Example of how this works:
Suppose you are crafting an enchant that requires Storm Dust, and Storm Dust costs 21g 70s on
the Auction House. CraftSim calculates your profit at 21g 44s per craft based on that price.

However, if you produce your own Storm Dust through a secondary craft such as Gleaming
Shattering, your actual cost per unit might be only 18g — significantly below the Auction House
price. By entering 18g as a Price Override for Storm Dust, CraftSim recalculates your profit
using your real material cost, which is considerably higher.

This is the core principle behind vertical integration in WoW crafting: crafting your own
intermediate materials at a lower cost than the Auction House price and using them as inputs
for your final crafts. Many crafts that appear unprofitable at Auction House material prices
become profitable when you supply your own cheaper reagents. This is why certain in-demand
items appear to have no margin — the crafters producing them are using self-sourced materials
that are not reflected in public pricing.


FEATURE: RECIPE SCAN
----------------------
Recipe Scan scans all of your known recipes and ranks them by profitability. This is the fastest
way to identify what you should be crafting in any given session.

IMPORTANT: Apply all your crafting buffs before running a Recipe Scan. If you scan without your
buffs active, the stat values used in the calculation will be incorrect and the results will
be misleading.

How to run a Recipe Scan:
1. Tick "Recipe Scan" in CraftSim to open the scan window.
2. Click the cogwheel icon next to the Scan Recipes button to open scan settings.
3. If you are crafting without concentration, leave the default settings.
   If you are crafting with concentration, also tick "Use Concentration" in the settings.
4. Click Scan Recipes.
5. Sort the results by "Relative Profit" if not using concentration, or by "Concentration Value"
   if using concentration.

Reading the results: Hover over any item in the list to see the required materials for that
craft. Left-clicking an item in the list will automatically select that recipe in your
Profession window.


FEATURE: CRAFT QUEUE
----------------------
To add a recipe to the Craft Queue, hold Shift and left-click the item in the Recipe Scan list.
The Craft Queue window will open and display all queued crafts along with a summary showing
your estimated total profit and total material cost across the entire queue.

Creating a shopping list from the queue: If you have the Auctionator addon installed, press the
"Create Auctionator Shopping List" button in the Craft Queue window. Then go to the Auction
House, open the Shopping tab, and find the "CraftSim CraftQueue" list on the left side.
Clicking it will scan for all required materials and automatically fill in the correct quantities
when you click each item to purchase it.

Note: The quantity auto-fill can behave unexpectedly when your queue includes crafts that
require different material ranks. Double-check the quantities manually if your queue contains
mixed-rank recipes.

After purchasing materials, you can craft directly through the CraftQueue interface or continue
crafting manually through the standard Profession window as usual.


ADDITIONAL FEATURES
--------------------
CraftSim includes some additional tools such as the ability to simulate crafting buffs rather
than requiring you to apply them before each scan. These are not necessary to get started and
can be explored once you are comfortable with the core features described above.


SUMMARY OF KEY POINTS
-----------------------
- Set price source to TradeSkillMaster for most reliable profit data.
- Enable Average Profit to factor in your actual profession stats.
- Use Simulation Mode to test profitability with materials you do not yet own.
- Use Specialization Info to confirm you are optimized for a given craft.
- Use Price Overrides when you craft your own materials cheaper than Auction House prices.
- Apply all crafting buffs before running a Recipe Scan.
- Sort Recipe Scan by Relative Profit (no concentration) or Concentration Value (with concentration).
- Use the Craft Queue and Auctionator Shopping List integration to streamline buying and crafting.
