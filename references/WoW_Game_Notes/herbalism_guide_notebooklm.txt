WORLD OF WARCRAFT: MIDNIGHT
HERBALISM LEVELING & FARMING GUIDE (1–100)
========================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This guide covers everything needed to level Herbalism from 1 to 100 and farm herbs efficiently
in Midnight. Topics include skill ranges, farming routes, profession equipment, consumables,
gathering stats, infused herbs, and the Overload mechanic.

Note: This guide focuses on leveling and farming only. Knowledge Point sources, treasure
locations, and specialization builds are covered in a separate guide.


WHAT CHANGED FROM THE WAR WITHIN
----------------------------------
If you gathered herbs in The War Within, the following things are different in Midnight.

Two Qualities Only: Herbs now only come in Silver and Gold quality. The Bronze quality tier has
been removed entirely.

Artisan Herbalist's Moxie: The shared Artisan's Acuity currency from The War Within has been
replaced by a profession-specific currency called Artisan Herbalist's Moxie, which can only
be spent on Herbalism-related items.

Epic Profession Tools: A new Epic quality tier of gathering tools is available. Epic tools have
more secondary stats than Rare (Blue) tools, but provide the same skill bonus.


HERBALISM TRAINER
------------------
You can learn Midnight Herbalism from Botanist Nathera, located in Silvermoon City.


PROFESSION EQUIPMENT
---------------------
Herbalism uses one Tool slot and two Accessory slots. All three slots have Green, Blue (Rare),
and Epic quality tiers.

Green quality items are tradeable (Bind on Equip) and can be purchased from the Auction House.
Blue and Epic quality items are crafted and Bind on Pickup, meaning they must be obtained
through Crafting Orders.

Accessory Slot 1 — Crafted by Tailoring:
- Green: Bright Linen Herbalism Hat
- Blue: Elegant Artisan's Herbalism Hat
- Epic: Thalassian Herbalist's Cowl

Accessory Slot 2 — Crafted by Leatherworking:
- Green: Eversong Botanist's Satchel
- Blue: Silvermoon Herbalist's Backpack
- Epic: Thalassian Herbtender's Cradle

Tool Slot — Crafted by Blacksmithing:
- Green: Thalassian Sickle
- Blue: Sun-Blessed Sickle
- Epic: Sunforged Sickle

Customizing Stats: Accessory stats are fixed, so simply aim for the highest quality you can
obtain. Tool stats can be customized using missives placed in the optional reagent slot when
the tool is crafted or when submitting a crafting order. Tools should also be enchanted.

Stat, Missive, and Enchant pairings:
- Deftness: Thalassian Missive of Deftness / Enchant Tool - Sin'dorei Deftness
- Finesse: Thalassian Missive of Finesse / Enchant Tool - Haranir Finesse
- Perception: Thalassian Missive of Perception / Enchant Tool - Amani Perception


GATHERING STATS: DEFTNESS, FINESSE, AND PERCEPTION
----------------------------------------------------
Deftness increases your gathering speed, which determines how quickly you complete the gather
and whether mobs can interrupt you before you finish.

Finesse gives you extra materials from each herb node.

Perception gives you a chance to find extra Nocturnal Lotus when one drops. It does not
increase the base drop chance of Nocturnal Lotus, only the bonus amount when one already drops.

Recommended stat priority for tools: Finesse or Deftness. Perception is generally not worth
prioritizing unless Nocturnal Lotus is selling for very high prices on the Auction House.

Because some Midnight zones have high mob density that makes it difficult to gather without
being interrupted, getting enough Deftness to gather safely is important. Using the consumables
listed below provides a comfortable Deftness baseline, after which you can stack Finesse. If
mobs are still interrupting you regularly, use a Deftness tool with a Deftness enchant as well.


CONSUMABLES
-----------
Using Azeroot Tea and Darkmoon Firewater significantly improves the gathering experience.
These are considered essential for effective farming until you have sufficient Deftness from
gear and specialization.

Haranir Phial of Perception: +38 Perception (+3.8%) and +11 Deftness (+3.6%). Lasts 30 minutes.
Haranir Phial of Finesse: +38 Finesse (+3.8%) and +11 Deftness (+3.6%). Lasts 30 minutes.
Argentleaf Tea: +50 Finesse (+5.0%). Lasts 1 hour.
Sanguithorn Tea: +50 Perception (+5.0%). Lasts 1 hour.
Azeroot Tea: +50 Deftness (+16.6%). Lasts 1 hour.
Darkmoon Firewater: +15% Deftness. Lasts 1 hour.
Refulgent Razorstone: +43 Finesse (+4.3%) specifically for gathering tools. Lasts 2 hours.


INFUSED HERBS AND OVERLOADING
-------------------------------
While gathering in Midnight, you will occasionally encounter infused versions of regular herbs.
These have a glowing visual effect. Gathering them drops extra materials in the form of motes,
but also applies a negative debuff effect to your character.

Learning Overload Infused Herb: The first time you gather any infused herb, you automatically
learn the Overload Infused Herb ability. This ability can be activated while near an infused
herb to trigger a bonus overload effect. The ability has a 12-hour cooldown, but the cooldown
is reduced by 30 minutes each time you gather any Midnight herb. This means gathering
approximately 24 herbs fully resets the cooldown.

If the Overload Infused Herb ability does not appear on your action bar automatically, find it
in your regular spellbook under the General tab. It is not listed in the Profession spellbook.

Infused Herb Types by Zone:
- Lightfused herbs are found primarily in Eversong Woods, with a few in Harandar. They drop Mote of Light.
- Wild herbs are found primarily in Zul'Aman. They drop Mote of Wild Magic.
- Primal herbs are found primarily in Harandar. They drop Mote of Primal Energy.
- Voidbound herbs are found primarily in Voidstorm, with a few in Eversong Woods and Zul'Aman. They drop Mote of Pure Void.

Overload Effects by Herb Type:

Lightfused — Normal gather: Light circles appear on the ground. Stand in them to collect extra
motes. Overload: More light circles spawn, but they are difficult to collect entirely so
expect to miss some.

Wild — Normal gather: Four herb creatures spawn. Kill them and use Herbalism on their corpses
to collect extra motes. Overload: An elite creature spawns instead. Killing it grants a
150 Perception buff for 5 minutes but provides no extra motes. The elite is difficult to
defeat with low gear.

Primal — Normal gather: A minor slow and damage-over-time effect is applied, nothing extra to
interact with. Overload: You channel 50% of your maximum HP to generate extra motes. Do not
interrupt the channel. Mobs do not appear to interrupt the channel, but the HP drain can kill
you if you begin the channel below 50% health.

Voidbound — Normal gather: A void zone pulls you toward it, but nothing significant happens.
Walk out and continue. Overload: A portal appears along with four purple orbs. Run into the
orbs quickly before they disappear to collect motes and ores. The portal itself teleports you
to random locations and is not useful in its current form.


SKILL RANGES AND WHAT TO GATHER
---------------------------------
Skill 1–30: Nearly everything gives skill points at this range. Tranquility Bloom stops
providing skill at 30, but all other herbs continue past that. Reaching 30 is fast.

Skill 30–60: The four main base herbs — Sanguithorn, Azeroot, Argentleaf, and Mana Lily —
go yellow (reduced skill gain) at 30 and grey (no skill gain) at 60. Lush and Infused herb
variants also provide skill points throughout this range.

Skill 60–100: All base herbs are grey and provide no skill. Only Lush and Infused herb variants
still give skill at this range, going yellow at 60 and grey at 100. These variants spawn
randomly in place of normal herbs, so continue gathering normally and you will encounter them.


FARMING ROUTES AND ZONE RECOMMENDATIONS
-----------------------------------------
Recommended starting zone: Eversong Woods. It has the most straightforward terrain and lower
mob density compared to other zones, making it easier to get comfortable with the gathering
flow before moving on.

Once you are familiar with the mechanics, move to other zones for variety and to collect
first-gather Knowledge Point bonuses from new herb types you have not gathered before.

The routes below are general guidelines, not strict paths to follow. Herbs also spawn outside
of the main routes, so exploring freely and finding your own paths is encouraged.

Eversong Woods: Best starting zone. Relatively open terrain, lower mob interference, and
Lightfused infused herbs that drop Mote of Light.

Zul'Aman: Contains Wild infused herbs that drop Mote of Wild Magic. Mob density is higher
than Eversong Woods.

Harandar: Contains Primal infused herbs that drop Mote of Primal Energy. Some areas make it
difficult to avoid mobs, so having enough Deftness to gather without being interrupted is
especially important here.

Voidstorm: Contains Voidbound infused herbs that drop Mote of Pure Void. This is the primary
zone for Voidbound herbs.
