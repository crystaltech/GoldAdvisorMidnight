WORLD OF WARCRAFT
HOW TO SELL TRANSMOG CORRECTLY
========================================
Last Updated: January 14, 2026

OVERVIEW
--------
Selling transmog (transmogrification items) on the Auction House is not straightforward, but
following the right process dramatically increases your sales and saves a significant amount
of time. This guide covers the essential addons, how to evaluate which items are worth posting,
and how to configure TradeSkillMaster correctly.

Credit: Transmog evaluation methods in this guide were taught by Methras.


REQUIRED ADDONS
----------------
You need both of these addons before you begin. There are no substitutes.

1. AllTheThings (ATT)
   A heavy addon but mandatory for transmog selling. It tells you whether an item has a unique
   appearance or shares its appearance with other items in the game.

2. TradeSkillMaster (TSM)
   Essential for posting efficiently when you have a large number of items. Without it, managing
   a transmog operation becomes impractical.


WHY PEOPLE BUY TRANSMOG
------------------------
Most transmog buyers are not purchasing items because they like how the item looks on their
character. They are buying to add the appearance to their wardrobe collection. WoW has a large
collector community, which means the visual appeal of an item to you is largely irrelevant —
what matters is whether a buyer needs it for their collection.


HOW TO EVALUATE ITEMS: UNIQUE VS SHARED APPEARANCE
----------------------------------------------------
Some items share the exact same appearance as other items. Collectors only need to obtain one
item per appearance, so shared-appearance items are harder to sell.

To check whether an item has a unique appearance, hover over it while the ATT addon is active.
ATT will list every item in the game that shares that appearance. If ATT lists more than one
item name, the item has a shared appearance. If ATT only lists that one item, it is unique.

Example of a shared appearance: ATT lists both "Warleader's Breastplate" and
"Adamantite Breastplate" when hovering either item. This means both items grant the same
wardrobe appearance, and collectors only need one of them.

Special case — treat as unique even if appearance is shared:
- If ATT shows a red dot before an item name, that item is Bind on Pickup (BoP). A BoP item
  cannot be traded, so your Auction House version may be the only obtainable source.
- If ATT shows a yellow dot before an item name, that item is a quest reward. Same logic applies.
In both cases, treat the item as unique regardless of whether it technically shares an appearance.


MINIMUM SELL RATE THRESHOLDS
------------------------------
To check an item's sell rate, hover over it with TSM active and look at the "Region Avg Daily
Sold" value. This tells you on average how many copies of that item sell per day across all
realms in the region.

Use these thresholds to decide whether to post an item:

- Shared appearance item: Post only if Region Avg Daily Sold is 0.04 or higher.
- Unique appearance item: Post if Region Avg Daily Sold is 0.02 or higher.

Items that fall below these thresholds are unlikely to sell and will waste your posting time.

Optional filter: Some sellers also set a minimum price floor, for example vendoring any item
worth less than 200 gold rather than posting it. This is a personal preference. When starting
out, it can be beneficial to post even cheap items to build up sales volume and learn what moves.


WHY NOT POST EVERYTHING?
--------------------------
Posting every item you find might seem like it would generate more sales overall, and while it
would add a small number of extra sales, the cost in time is not worth it. As your Auction House
inventory grows, posting takes increasingly long. Filtering to only items that meet the thresholds
above keeps your operation manageable and focused on items that have a realistic chance of selling.


CRITICAL TSM SETTING: ADD ITEMS AS BASE ITEMS
----------------------------------------------
This is the single most important configuration step. If you do not add your transmog items to
your TSM group as "Base Items," your transmog operation will not succeed.

What Base Item means: When an item is added as a Base Item, TSM compares your listing against
all other copies of that item regardless of stats, secondary stats, or item level. This is
correct behavior for transmog because buyers do not care about stats or item level — they only
want the appearance. Without this setting, TSM may treat your item as a different product from
identical-looking items with different stats, causing pricing and comparison to work incorrectly.

How to add items as Base Items: When adding items to your TSM group, select "Base Item" from
the item type option. If you have already added items without choosing Base Item, you must
remove them from the group and re-add them with the Base Item setting selected.


TSM AUCTIONING OPERATION
--------------------------
A simple Auctioning operation is sufficient for posting transmog. You do not need a complex
setup to get started. If you are unfamiliar with TSM operations, refer to a dedicated TSM
beginner guide before proceeding, as understanding the basics will make this much easier.

The operation settings used as a baseline for this guide are straightforward and not heavily
optimized, but they are functional and will get items listed correctly.


POSTING SPEED TIP: MULTI-REALM ROTATION
-----------------------------------------
After posting a large number of items, the Auction House interface will begin to lag noticeably.
When this happens, log out and switch to a character on a different realm, then post the items
stored on that character. Rotating between characters on different realms resets the lag and
significantly improves your posting speed.

Note: This technique is only relevant if you are selling transmog across multiple servers.


FINAL NOTES
-----------
Transmog selling is not the fastest or most exciting gold-making method, but following this
process — correct addons, appearance evaluation, sell rate thresholds, and the Base Item
setting — will meaningfully increase the number of items you sell compared to posting without
a system.
